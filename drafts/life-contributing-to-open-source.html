My pytest Contribution

Posted by Aly Sivji in _currently-working-on   

I stumbled across Peter Norvig's Teach Yourself Programming in Ten Years essay right when I got serious about learning how to code a couple of years back. I had few failed attempted under my belt, but I couldn't seem to make the habit stick. Being early in my career, I was fixated on the learning the latest technologies everybody was talking about. JavaScript, Python, Data Science, Hadoop, Spark, Cassandra, AWS, Mongo, you name it. I went to a lot of meetups; I wasn't focused nor disciplined in my approach to learning.

Reading this essay helped me understand that it's not about learning how to do everything at once. Learning how to code is a process and requires deliberate practice. You need to challenge yourself with tasks that are just beyond your current ability. No single programmer knows it all, not even the "rock stars" you worship on Twitter. Good programmers do share one characteristic: they know how to solve problems. The act of constantly challenging yourself with projects you don't quite know how to do forces you to git gud.

I took Peter's advice to heart: I wrote a lot of code, I read a lot of code, and I worked on projects beyond my abilities. I also started contributing to Open Source as I found this to be one of the best ways to practice. Digging into an unfamiliar code base in order to fix a bug or add a feature is rarely trivial, especially when working on a mature project used by millions of people.

A couple of weeks ago, pytest 4.1.0 was released. There were a lot of changes in this minor release including my first contribution, a PR that that took four months to get merged. In this post, I will talk the change before doing a quick retrospective on the entire experience (wc?).

pytest
